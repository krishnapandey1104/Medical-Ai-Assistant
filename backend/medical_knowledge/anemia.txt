Anemia Overview

Anemia is a condition in which the body does not have enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to tissues. It often causes fatigue and weakness.

Common Causes

Iron deficiency  
Vitamin B12 deficiency  
Chronic diseases  
Blood loss  
Bone marrow disorders

Symptoms

Fatigue  
Weakness  
Pale or yellowish skin  
Shortness of breath  
Dizziness  
Cold hands and feet

Normal Hemoglobin Levels

Men: 13.5 – 17.5 g/dL  
Women: 12.0 – 15.5 g/dL  
Children: 11 – 16 g/dL

Diagnosis

Doctors usually diagnose anemia through blood tests such as a complete blood count (CBC).

Management

Treatment depends on the cause and may include dietary improvements, supplements, or medical treatment under professional guidance.
