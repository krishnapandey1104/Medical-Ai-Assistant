Blood Pressure Overview

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of arteries as the heart pumps blood. It is measured using two numbers: systolic pressure and diastolic pressure.

Systolic pressure represents the pressure when the heart beats. Diastolic pressure represents the pressure when the heart rests between beats.

Normal Blood Pressure Levels

Normal: Less than 120/80 mmHg  
Elevated: 120–129 systolic and less than 80 diastolic  
Hypertension Stage 1: 130–139 systolic or 80–89 diastolic  
Hypertension Stage 2: 140 or higher systolic or 90 or higher diastolic  
Hypertensive Crisis: 180/120 mmHg or higher

Symptoms of High Blood Pressure

High blood pressure often has no symptoms. However, very high blood pressure may cause headaches, chest pain, shortness of breath, or nosebleeds.

Risk Factors

High salt intake  
Obesity  
Smoking  
Lack of physical activity  
Family history  
Stress

Prevention and Management

Maintaining a healthy weight  
Regular physical activity  
Reducing salt intake  
Eating fruits and vegetables  
Managing stress  
Regular health check-ups
