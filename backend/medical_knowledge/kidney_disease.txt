Kidney Disease Overview

Kidneys filter waste and excess fluids from the blood. Chronic kidney disease occurs when the kidneys gradually lose their ability to function properly.

Common Causes

Diabetes  
High blood pressure  
Kidney infections  
Genetic conditions  
Long-term use of certain medications

Symptoms

Fatigue  
Swelling in legs and ankles  
Changes in urination  
Shortness of breath  
Nausea

Kidney Function Tests

Creatinine Test: Measures waste levels in blood  
GFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate): Estimates kidney function

Normal GFR: 90 or higher  
Mild kidney damage: 60 – 89  
Moderate damage: 30 – 59  
Severe damage: below 30

Management

Controlling blood pressure  
Managing diabetes  
Healthy diet  
Reducing salt intake  
Regular medical monitoring
