Liver Disease Overview

The liver is an important organ responsible for detoxification, metabolism, and nutrient storage. Liver diseases affect the liver's ability to function properly.

Common Types of Liver Disease

Fatty Liver Disease
Fatty liver occurs when excess fat accumulates in liver cells. It may be associated with obesity, diabetes, and alcohol consumption.

Hepatitis
Hepatitis refers to inflammation of the liver. It may be caused by viruses such as hepatitis A, B, and C.

Cirrhosis
Cirrhosis is severe scarring of the liver resulting from long-term liver damage.

Symptoms of Liver Disease

Fatigue  
Yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice)  
Abdominal pain or swelling  
Nausea  
Loss of appetite  
Dark urine  

Risk Factors

Excess alcohol consumption  
Obesity  
Viral infections  
Certain medications  
Diabetes

Prevention

Healthy diet  
Limiting alcohol consumption  
Vaccination for hepatitis  
Maintaining a healthy weight  
Regular medical checkups
